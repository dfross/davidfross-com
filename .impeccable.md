## Design Context

### Users
- Potential clients, employers, and fellow developers.
- Goal: Evaluate David's technical prowess and design sensibility, read in-depth case studies, and initiate contact.
- Context: Professional evaluation, often from a workspace or a dev-focused context.

### Brand Personality
- Technical, Peaceful, and Minimal.
- Voice: Precise, humble, and competent. David is a "maker" of high-performance digital products and websites.
- Emotional Goals: Trust, peace, and clarity. The visitor should feel that the site is built with extreme care and technical precision.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Visual Tone: Dark, minimal, and high-contrast "Vercel-style" aesthetics.
- References: Vercel (minimalism, focus on typography/whitespace, subtle gradients).
- Anti-references: Terminal looks, over-the-top flashy animations, or generic "AI slop" styles (glowing glassmorphism).
- Theme: Deep neutrals (neutral-900+) with crisp typography (IBM Plex Sans).

### Design Principles
1. **Precision & Performance**: Every element must be intentional. Layouts should be mathematically sound, and performance (loading/interaction) should be near-instant.
2. **Accessible by Design**: Accessibility isn't an afterthought; it's a core feature. High contrast, clear focus states, and semantic HTML are non-negotiable.
3. **Typography First**: Content is king. Use IBM Plex Sans with generous whitespace to ensure readability and a technical, yet approachable feel.
4. **Subtle Delight**: Use Motion for quiet, sophisticated interactions that reinforce the feeling of "Peace" rather than "Noise." Avoid visual competition.
